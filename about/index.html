About me

I love programming and software engineering in general. I have significant professional experience programming in C++ and other languages. I like working with low level features and metaprogramming, but I also enjoy learning and applying language-independent concepts and patterns to my work. I can do my job with minimal input and maximal independence, but I don't really like the lone rider programmer clichè: great software is only built by teams of people!

Computer Vision

During my first two years working at GRADIANT, they taught me a lot about facial feature tracking. We implemented our own version of a Mean-Shift based Constrained Local Model (CLM; in the fashion of [Saragih2009], as well as other types of CLM and AAM -based systems. My Master's Thesis (2013) summarises most of work in face tracking and expression recognition.

Later on, I have worked in video and scene analysis projects

Software
